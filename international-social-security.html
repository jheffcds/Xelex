International Social Security

At Xelex we can assist with all International Social Security elements of an international assignment. International social security advice refers to guidance and information provided to individuals regarding their social security rights and obligations when they move between countries or work in multiple countries. This can include advice on:


  1. Bilateral Agreements: Understanding treaties or agreements that exist between countries that can affect social security benefits, such as totalization agreements which prevent double taxation of social security.
  2. Retirement Benefits: Understanding how to qualify for retirement benefits in different countries and how the benefits from one country may affect those from another.
  3. Disability Benefits: Information about accessing disability payments and how to apply while living or working in another country.
  4. Health Care: Guidance on health insurance coverage and medical benefits when living abroad or traveling.
  5. Pension Accumulation: How pensions are accrued and transferred internationally, including bilateral agreements between countries.
  6. Tax Implications: Insight into potential tax obligations related to social security benefits and working in multiple jurisdictions.
  7. Eligibility Requirements: Information on the requirements for qualifying for benefits in different countries, including work history and contributions.
