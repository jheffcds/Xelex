Posted Workers Notification

In European Union, there is a system in place that allows for the free movement of services and workers. Workers residing in one EU Member State can offer their services on behalf of their employer in another Member State. To protect workers' social rights and prevent social dumping, the EU introduced the revised Posted Workers Directive in 2018. This Directive establishes essential employment terms and conditions that must be adhered to in alignment with the regulations of the host Member State, provided these are more advantageous than those offered by the employment laws of the home country or the applicable employment contract law. Before posted workers commence their services in any EU Member State, they must be reported to the relevant host country authorities in advance. Similarly, under the Swiss Posted Workers Act, posted workers in Switzerland must be reported to the local cantonal authorities at least eight days before starting work on Swiss territory. At Xelex, we can help clarify the Posted Workers Notification process and assist with submissions in both Switzerland and EU Member States, ensuring you avoid compliance issues and hefty fines.
