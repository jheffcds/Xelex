Switzerland

1-8 days
In principle no permit is required, but for some nationals a business visa to enter the Schengen Area (including Switzerland) will be need
9-90 days
Online notification: At least 8 days prior to start working on the Swiss territory an online notification process is in place similar to the posted workers notification in the EU. This simplified process allows any EU/EFTA- based company to assign their employees to work in Switzerland for a maximum of 90 working days per calendar year. The allocation of the 90 days is done per legal entity, per assignee, and per calendar year.
This option is limited to the following groups of individuals:
  1. EU/EFTA nationals working in Switzerland for a maximum of 90 days.

  2. Posted workers from a company established in the EU/EFTA, regardless of their nationality.
It is important to note that posted workers must have been integrated into the EU/EFTA labour market for at least one year prior to being posted to Switzerland.
91-120 days
The 4-month permit or 120 sporadic days permit typically allows for a fast- track process, depending on the Swiss canton, which involves fewer formalities and does not require registration in Switzerland.
For the 120 sporadic days permit, all days spent in Switzerland are counted over a period of 12 months, including weekends, public holidays, and vacations. One drawback of the 4-month permit is that in most cantons, an extension is not possible, and a “cooling-off” period of at least 12 months outside Swiss territory is required.
Meanwhile, a disadvantage of the 120 days permit is that tracking the 120 days can be cumbersome, and only one 120-day permit can be granted.
121-365 days
An L-permit (short-term permit) is required, and there are quotas in place for this type of permit (with the exception of EU/EFTA nationals employed under a local Swiss employment contract).
The L-permit is valid for a maximum of 12 months and can be extended for up to 24 months for non-EEA/non-EFTA nationals. After 24 months, the L-permit will be converted to a B-permit.
It is crucial for the L-permit holder to register with the local Swiss municipality of residence within 14 days of their arrival and, in any case, prior to commencing work in Switzerland.
The permit will be forfeited after more than 3 consecutive months of absence from Switzerland
1-5 years
A B-permit (long-term permit) is required, and there are quotas in place for this type of permit (with the exception of EU/EFTA nationals employed under a local Swiss employment contract). This category of permit is usally granted for assignments above 2 years or foreign employees working under a local Swiss employment contract. Extension of this permit is possible and depends on nationality and reason of stay.
It is crucial for the B-permit holder to register with the local Swiss municipality of residence within 14 days of their arrival and, in any case, prior to commencing work in Switzerland. The permit will be forfeited after more than 6 consecutive months of absence from Switzerland. This type of permit can be frozen for up to 4 years in case the employee has a valid reason for a temporary stay abroad (e.g. military service, work,...)
+10 years
After ten years of continuous legal residence in Switzerland, the employee becomes eligible for a C-permit (permanent residency status), provided they meet specific conditions such as integration and language proficiency. Additionally, certain EU/EFTA nationals may also be granted a C-permit after five years of residency in Switzerland, subject to meeting certain criteria
Cross border commuters
A G-permit is issued to foreign nationals who are employed in Switzerland under a local Swiss employment contract while residing abroad.
The permit's validity corresponds with the employment contract's duration, or it can last for up to five years in the case of a contract with indefinite duration. The G-permit is not subject to quota restrictions and is renewable. Holders of a G-permit are required to return to their home country at least once a week. EU/EFTA nationals with residency in the entire EU/EFTA area are eligible for the G-permit. For non-EU nationals, a G-permit may only be granted if they have permanent resident status in a neighboring country and have resided in the border zone with Switzerland for at least six months. Priority will be given to Swiss and EU/EFTA nationals, and a salary assessment will be conducted.
