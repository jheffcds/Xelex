Belgium

With over 25 years of experience at Xelex, we offer comprehensive legal assistance concerning the employment and residence of foreign nationals in Belgium. Additionally, we can help you acquire the required permits for self-employment and support you in establishing a company based in Belgium.
Belgium is an attractive location for international business, partly due to its highly productive workforce. However, if you're looking to relocate employees or hire new staff from abroad, it's essential to ensure that all team members have the necessary visas and permits to work legally.
Nationals of the European Union or the European Economic Area (EEA) are exempted from holding a work permit to work on the Belgian territory. However, for stay longer than 90 days these nationals need to register at the local municipality (gemeentehuis / maison communal) and obtain a residence permit.

Types of Work Visas in Belgium

Belgium offers three categories of work permits for foreign nationals:

  • Work Permit Type A: To qualify for this permit, the applicant must have held a Type B permit for four years during an unbroken ten-year period in Belgium. Type A permits are valid indefinitely.
  • Work Permit Type B: This permit allows foreign nationals to fill positions that Belgian nationals cannot occupy. It is valid for one year and can be renewed.
  • Work Permit Type C: This permit is granted to students and other temporary visitors and is valid for a maximum of one year.

Type B is the most commonly issued work permit, and it is likely the one your employees will need. Along with a work permit, employees must obtain a residence visa to live in Belgium.
Requirements for Obtaining Belgian Work Visas
As Belgium is a member of the European Union (EU), nationals from other EU member states can work there without needing a work permit. Additionally, citizens from the European Economic Area (EEA) and Switzerland are exempt as well. However, other foreign nationals will require a work permit.
It is the employer's responsibility to secure a Type B work permit for foreign workers.
