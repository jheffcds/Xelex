Expat Policies

At Xelex we are experienced lawyers in drafting tailor-made expat policies for our clients. An expat policy is a set of guidelines and procedures that an organisation establishes for managing employees who temporarily or permanently move abroad for work. This policy aims to support expatriates in their transition to a new cultural and professional environment. Several elements often included in an expat policy are:


  1. Relocation and settlement support: Assistance with housing, relocation services and finding a new home.
  2. Financial aspects: Information on salary, taxes, insurance and any living allowances or allowances.
  3. Cultural training: Trainings and resources to help expats adjust to the new culture and environment.
  4. Healthcare and insurance: Information on healthcare and available insurance abroad.
  5. Family support: Assistance for expat family members, such as education for children and work opportunities for partners.

A well-executed expat policy contributes to the well-being of expats and their families, and can also increase the success rate of international assignments.
